My First Letter To You

Hi...

It would take a hundred words to make a decent attempt at conveying who you are. It would take at least thirty more to describe what you mean to me. I talk a lot, but it baffles me still just how difficult I find it to describe you. The way that you talk induces a joyous sensation that travels from my ear ti the tips of my fingers. The vibrations imitate the sound of a guitar strum at mild tempo. The sustenance in thw tone of your voice need no description but the weight of it does, the sense that I'm floating across a still ocean as I lay buoyant and the 3 words that make me feel glad I'm not perfect reverberate through the nodes of my brain. Because perfect is a lie, but you and I transcend the need for such inscriptions.

A letter above a hundred and more words be evident of the warmth you bring and the cold you druve away. The day I met wasn't the happiest day of my life, and I'm sorry to be so blunt with that piece of information. But it is a memory I dread to take away because what's special is what I got to feel as I watched you struggle with the presence of the man in front of you. You probably asked yourself questionsin hopes to compare what you knew and thought, to what you saw and got, like, is this him? I don't have a hundred words, but I can put a couple of sentences together and hope for the best in that the best makes you feel even a fraction of happiness as you have come to know it.
